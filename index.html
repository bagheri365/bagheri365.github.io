About

Alireza Bagheri is a Vice President Data Scientist at JPMorgan Chase & Co., 5 Manhattan West, New York, NY 10001. Prior to that, he was a Senior Machine Learning Engineer at Roots Automation, 85 Broad St, New York, NY 10004, and he was a Machine Learning Engineer at the American Express AI Labs, 200 Vesey Street, New York, NY. He received a PhD degree in Electrical Engineering from New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT), Newark, New Jersey in 2019. He received an MSc degree (with first-class honors) in Electrical Engineering from Semnan University, Semnan, Iran in 2013.

Dr. Bagheri's research interests include Wireless Communication, Optimization, and Machine Learning. His overall PhD research goal was the establishment of a theoretical framework to enable the design of flexible Bio-inspired computing algorithms that are tailored to the solution of supervised and unsupervised cognitive tasks. In particular, he worked on supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and adversarial training for Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs). As part of his PhD and Master degrees, he also worked on Mobile Cloud Computing (MCC), and Cognitive Radio Networks (CRNs). Most of his research works funded by grants from leading agencies such as the National Science Foundation (NSF) of US, the European Research Council (ERC), the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) of UK, the National Foundation for Science and Technology (NAFOSTED) of Vietnam, the Academy of Finland, the TEKES of Finland, and the Research Program DGRES of Greece.

Dr. Bagheri serves as a regular reviewer for IEEE Communications Letters, IET Communications Journal, IEEE International Conference on Communications (ICC), IEEE Wireless Communications & Networking Conference (WCNC), IEEE International Symposium on Personal, Indoor and Mobile Radio Communications (PIMRC), and IEEE Vehicular Technology Conference (VTC).
